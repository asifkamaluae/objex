Why Perpetual Trading?

The first deployment market is an architectural decision.

OBJEX is fundamentally a monthly recurring capital formation protocol. The Obligation Engine imposes four requirements on any market it deploys into:

Continuous liquidity
Capital must deploy and unwind at any moment in the cycle.
Continuous settlement
Obligations settle on protocol time, not market hours.
Continuous accounting
Positions must be marked and audited in real time.
Continuous obligations
Contribution schedules run every month, without pause.
Liquid financial markets naturally support all four — so perpetual trading becomes the first deployment market. Future expansion will occur only into markets whose liquidity characteristics remain compatible with the protocol.

Risk & Solvency

Why this is not undercollateralized lending.

Who takes the loss?
A member can only lose their own paid-in capital plus realized profit. Community principal is ring-fenced and can never be drawn below secured contributions.
What is the collateral?
Every unit of buying power is backed 1:1 by committed, on-chain contribution obligations held in escrow — not by an external lender's balance sheet.
Why not just lending?
No interest, no rehypothecation, no counterparty. Capital originates from the community and is capped in code — the protocol cannot extend risk beyond secured capital.
§
Solvency is a property of the code, not a promise of the operator. Exposure can never exceed capital the protocol already holds.
